Snapshot date: 2026-07-03

Rule: do not build a SaaS, course, dashboard, agency site, or large automation before proving repeated demand. Start with manual services, sell the fix, document the result, then automate only repeated steps.

## Evidence To Collect Before Expanding

For each service idea, collect at least 10 proof points:

- public job/freelance posts mentioning the problem,
- Upwork/Fiverr/Freelancer search results,
- direct messages where prospects describe the issue,
- calls with founders or small agencies,
- support forum posts from users with the same issue,
- paid trial work,
- repeat requests from one client.

## Validation Interview Questions

Use these before building anything bigger than the current toolkit:

1. What is running on your VPS right now?
2. What broke recently or worries you most?
3. How do you currently know when the app is down?
4. Where are your backups stored?
5. Have you ever tested a restore?
6. What would happen if the server failed today?
7. Who currently fixes deployment issues?
8. What would a successful 48-hour outcome look like?
9. What have you already tried?
10. Would you pay a fixed price for a written diagnosis, fix, and handover?

## Validation Thresholds

Build only small scripts/docs when:
- 5+ prospects mention the same pain,
- 2+ people agree to a call or detailed chat,
- 1+ person pays or offers a testimonial for the exact service,
- the work creates reusable evidence for jobs.

Consider a recurring maintenance offer when:
- 3+ clients ask for ongoing checks,
- at least 2 clients pay for the same monthly task,
- the work can be delivered with a checklist in under 2 hours/month/client.

Do not build SaaS until:
- 10+ buyers describe the same repeated workflow,
- 3+ buyers say they would pay monthly,
- at least 1 buyer pays for a manual version,
- the manual process is stable enough to automate.
