# Tracker Guide

These CSV files are meant to stay simple enough for GitHub, Excel, Google Sheets, or LibreOffice.

Use them to answer three questions every week:

1. Are you applying to the right jobs?
2. Are buyers replying to the right service offer?
3. Is the current plan producing proof, interviews, or paid work?

## Files

### `daily-application-tracker.csv`

Use this for every role you review seriously, especially when you apply.

Recommended habits:

- fill `Match Score 1-5` before applying,
- update `Status` after each action,
- set `Portfolio Link Sent` to `Yes` when you include CloudOps Rescue Kit,
- keep `Next Action` concrete.

Weekly checks:

- how many applications were sent,
- how many were strong-fit (`4` or `5`) roles,
- how many required Docker, Linux, cloud support, monitoring, or troubleshooting,
- how many moved to recruiter reply or interview.

### `freelance-lead-validation-tracker.csv`

Use this for job posts, direct leads, founder conversations, and marketplace searches.

Recommended habits:

- record the exact buyer wording in `Problem Mentioned`,
- use `Proof Captured` when you saved screenshots or copied language into notes,
- keep `Service Fit` tied to one package unless the lead clearly spans more,
- record a next action even when the answer is "no pitch yet".

Weekly checks:

- how many qualified leads were found,
- which package appeared most often,
- which problems were urgent enough to justify fixed-price work,
- whether buyers asked for recurring help.

### `client-intake-call-notes.csv`

Use this after a DM turns into a real conversation.

Capture:

- scope boundaries,
- delivery deadline,
- user-impact level,
- backup state,
- exact risk language from the buyer,
- package fit or reason to decline.

### `weekly-execution-scoreboard.csv`

Use one row per week to summarize the entire 90-day push.

Track:

- applications,
- interviews,
- outreach volume,
- reply rate,
- qualified leads,
- proof artifacts published,
- revenue in DA or USD,
- the top blocker for the next week.

## Weekly Review Rule

Do a 20-minute review at the end of each week.

Keep doing what increases one of these:

- recruiter replies,
- interviews,
- qualified buyer conversations,
- paid trial work,
- publishable proof.

Cut or pause actions that create activity without signal.
